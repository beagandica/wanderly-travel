# Seoul itinerary: 2 days

## Traveler profile

- **Vibe**: Foodie + Culture
- **Budget**: Mix ($ to $$$)
- **Focus**: Historic neighborhoods, legendary street food, palace culture, local markets

---

## Day 1: Old Seoul - Markets, Palaces & Soul Food

### 🌅 Morning

**Gyeongbokgung Palace** (경복궁), Jongno
Rent a hanbok (traditional Korean dress) and feel like royalty wandering through Seoul's grandest palace.
This UNESCO site dates back centuries and the morning light hits the architecture perfectly.
💰 Budget: $ (free entry with hanbok rental)
🚇 How to get there: Subway Line 3, Gyeongbokgung Station (Exit 5)

### ☀️ Afternoon

**Bukchon Hanok Village** (북촌한옥마을), Bukchon
Just a 10-minute walk downhill from the palace, this is where 600-year-old wooden hanok houses line quiet, hilly streets.
Instagram gold, but visit midweek morning to escape the crowds.
💰 Budget: Free
🚇 How to get there: Walk downhill from Gyeongbokgung or take Line 3 to Anguk Station

**🍜 Lunch: Tosokchon Samgyetang** (토속촌), Jongno
Ginseng chicken soup that tastes like a thousand grandmothers' love.
One spoonful and you'll understand why people line up for hours.
Order the samgyetang and pair it with the silky side dish of roasted perilla leaves.
💰 Budget: $$
🚇 How to get there: 5-minute walk from Bukchon

### 🌙 Evening

**Gwangjang Market** (광장마켓), Jongno
The energy here is *electric*, neon signs, rapid-fire Korean conversations, the smell of bindaetteok crisping in hot oil.
This is where Seoulites actually eat, not where tourists go.
💰 Budget: $
🚇 How to get there: Subway Line 1, Jongno Station; walk to market entrance

**🍽️ Dinner: Street Food Experience at Gwangjang**
Order bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes, crispy and savory), mayak gimbap (the "addiction" kimbap), and yukhoe (raw beef tartare).
Wash it down with a makgeolli (rice wine) if you're feeling adventurous.
Try asking the ajumma (Korean grandmother vendor) "하나 더 주세요" (hana deo juseyo: one more please).
💰 Budget: $

## Day 2: Culture Deep-Dive & Hidden Food Gems

### 🌅 Morning

**Changdeokgung Secret Garden** (창덕궁), Jongno
This palace has what Gyeongbokgung doesn't: a *secret garden* (Biwon) that feels like stepping into a painting.
Guided tours only, so book the day before.
The moss-covered paths, ancient trees, and hidden pavilions are UNESCO World Heritage for a reason.
💰 Budget: $$
🚇 How to get there: Subway Line 3, Anguk Station; 10-minute walk

**🍽️ Brunch: Cafe Onion** (카페어니언), Ikseon-dong
This converted hanok is the most photogenic cafe in Seoul, hands down.
Order their iconic pandoro bread with a cream latte while sitting in a 200-year-old wooden house.
One sentence per line, traveler.
💰 Budget: $$
🚇 How to get there: Subway Line 3, Jongno 3-ga Station; 8-minute walk

### ☀️ Afternoon

**Tongin Market** (통인시장), Jongno
Buy brass coins at the entrance, then fill your tray at different stalls, tteokbokki, japchae, fried items, whatever looks good.
It's like a lunch adventure designed specifically for you.
This is a *real* market where locals shop, not a tourist attraction.
💰 Budget: $
🚇 How to get there: Subway Line 3, Jongno 3-ga Station; 5-minute walk from Cafe Onion

### 🌙 Evening

**Cheonggyecheon Stream** (청계천), Central Seoul
A restored urban stream running through downtown that transforms into pure magic at night with ambient lighting.
Walk along the water, grab street snacks, watch the city slow down.
💰 Budget: Free
🚇 How to get there: Subway Line 5, Gwanghwamun Station; follow signs to the stream

**🍽️ Dinner: Myeongdong Kyoja** (명동교자), Myeongdong
Legendary kalguksu (knife-cut noodles) in a broth perfected over decades.
Cash only, tiny cramped space, zero pretension, 100% authenticity.
The dumplings (mandu) are enormous; the noodles slip down your throat like silk.
Say "맛있어요!" (mashisseoyo!) to the owner, they *love* this.
💰 Budget: $
🚇 How to get there: Subway Line 4, Myeongdong Station (Exit 6); 3-minute walk

## Practical notes

- **T-money card**: Buy at any GS25 or CU convenience store (about 2,500 KRW). Load it with cash and tap for subway, buses, and some restaurants.
- **Key apps**: Download Naver Map or KakaoMap (Google Maps doesn't work reliably in Korea). Set locations in Korean for best results.
- **Currency**: 1 USD ≈ 1,300 KRW. Many vendors don't take cards, so carry cash.
- **Local phrase of the trip**: "계산해 주세요" (gyesanhae juseyo: the bill please), you'll need this after every meal in blissful Seoul.

## Why this itinerary works

**Day 1** takes you through Old Seoul's iconic palace, serene hanok village, and into the *real* Seoul of grandmothers and markets.
**Day 2** dives deeper into palace culture with Changdeokgung's secret garden, pairs it with trendy cafe culture, and ends with authentic noodle comfort.
You're eating where locals eat, walking where they walk, and soaking up a place that somehow bridges 600 years and modern K-pop obsession in the same breath.
The neighborhoods flow naturally to minimize subway time, leaving more time for the important stuff: food, wandering, and falling in love with Seoul.
